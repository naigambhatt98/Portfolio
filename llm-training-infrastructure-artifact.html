01 — Training Pipeline

From raw data to deployment

Each company uses its own stack, but the path is consistent: gather massive datasets, clean and tokenize them, train a transformer at scale, evaluate behavior, align outputs, then deploy. Every stage has its own cost signature.

— 01

Data Collection

Teams gather text, code, and multimodal content from web crawls, books, repositories, licensed corpora, and increasingly from synthetic generation pipelines.

— 02

Cleaning & Filtering

Low-quality pages, duplicates, unsafe content, spam, and formatting noise are reduced until the training set becomes genuinely useful.

— 03

Tokenization

Text is split into machine-readable tokens — a step that directly shapes memory usage, sequence length, and training efficiency across the whole run.

— 04

Pretraining

The model learns statistical structure in language by predicting tokens across huge distributed accelerator clusters. This is usually the dominant compute expense.

— 05

Post-Training

Instruction tuning, preference learning, safety alignment, and evaluations transform a capable but raw model into a useful and appropriately constrained system.

— 06

Deployment

After optimization, the model enters products and APIs. Infrastructure costs shift from training expense toward inference cost, latency management, and reliability.

Very High Impact

Distributed Training

Massive jobs must synchronize across thousands of chips with minimal idle time — a fundamental systems engineering challenge.

High Impact

Data Quality Management

Better data often matters as much as more data. The filtering pipeline defines model ceiling as much as model size does.

High Impact

Fault Tolerance

Long runs need checkpointing, monitoring, and automatic recovery. A single failure in a weeks-long job is extremely costly.

Medium Impact

Alignment & Safety

Capability alone is insufficient. Models must be useful, controllable, and safe — requiring dedicated datasets and compute cycles.

02 — Resource Categories

The four forces that define cost

🗂️

Data

The cost extends far beyond gathering content. Licensing, storage, filtering pipelines, annotation labor, red-teaming datasets, and synthetic data generation all accumulate into a substantial infrastructure layer.

Relative CostMedium
🖥️

Compute

Accelerator clusters — GPUs, TPUs — execute the training job. At frontier scale this is almost always the dominant cost. Parallel hardware demand is extreme and the hardware is expensive to lease or own.

Relative CostVery High
⚡

Energy

Power consumption scales with cluster size, cooling infrastructure, and job duration. Energy efficiency now matters for operating cost and environmental impact alike — and is increasingly a public concern.

Relative CostHigh
⏳

Time

Training is only part of the total timeline. Evaluation, tuning cycles, safety checks, infrastructure setup, and deployment readiness all extend the development schedule — and engineering time is never free.

04 — Cost Economics

Why the bill keeps climbing

Compute cluster leasing or ownership 95

Largest direct cost for frontier runs. Accelerator time is at a premium and jobs demand thousands of chips in parallel.

Energy and cooling 74

Power draw is substantial when large clusters run continuously across training windows of weeks or months.

Deployment and inference readiness 69

Models must be optimized for latency, throughput, global scaling, and monitoring before they serve millions of requests.

Post-training and evaluation 63

Quality, safety, and consistency require compute cycles beyond raw pretraining — plus dedicated human evaluation labor.

05 — Key Findings

What this all means

LLM performance is not purely a machine learning story. It is equally an infrastructure story, a systems design story, and a cost optimization story. Each of these dimensions shapes what is actually buildable.

📚

Data Quality Shapes Capability

More data helps, but better data often lifts model quality more efficiently than simply scaling raw volume. The filtering pipeline is a first-class engineering problem.

🧠

Pretraining Is Only One Phase

Instruction tuning, preference learning, red-teaming, and evaluation are essential before any public release. The pretraining run is the beginning, not the end.

🏭

Infrastructure Determines Feasibility

Without large accelerator clusters, high-bandwidth networking, fault-tolerant storage, and careful job orchestration, training at frontier scale is simply not practical.

🌍

Efficiency Will Define the Next Era

As models grow, cost, energy, and environmental pressure will push the industry toward more efficient training architectures, smaller models, and smarter data strategies.
